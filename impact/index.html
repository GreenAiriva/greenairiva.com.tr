The Measurement Distinction

Modelled vs Measured: Why It Matters to Your Auditor.

Most emission reduction claims rely on models — assumptions about what a technology should achieve under defined conditions. Models are useful for projections but increasingly inadequate for compliance reporting, where regulators and auditors want to see what actually happened.

GreenAiriva reports what was actually captured — in mass, per unit, per interval. For high-GWP gases like nitrous oxide (N₂O, GWP ≈ 273) and fluorinated compounds (GWP reaching several thousand), mass-based verification is especially critical: a small variance in reported mass translates into a large variance in declared CO₂e. That distinction is becoming the dividing line in CBAM declarations, TSRS-ESG audits, and municipal climate account reviews.

Modelled Approach
  • Based on assumed reference conditions
  • Cannot account for real-world variability
  • Difficult to third-party audit
  • Increasingly rejected in CBAM and ESG contexts
Mass-Based Measurement
  • Reflects what actually occurred
  • Unit-level, timestamped, traceable
  • Full third-party audit access
  • Directly integrable with compliance frameworks
