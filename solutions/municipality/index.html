The Municipal Challenge

Municipalities are accountable for their city's cumulative emission footprint — not just their own buildings, but the entire urban area.

Passive Methods Can't Reach Street-Level Emissions

Fleet electrification, solar installations, and reforestation require high upfront investment and cannot directly intervene in uncontrolled street-level emissions — particularly the ~35% share attributable to road transport. These approaches remain passive. GreenAiriva captures actively, at the source, where the pollution actually occurs.

Pledges Are No Longer Enough — Proof Is Required

Net Zero 2053 and municipal Climate Action Plans now demand measurable, documented reduction — not procurement changes or letters of intent. Citizens, councils, and TSRS-ESG auditors accept verifiable outcomes, not modelled estimates. Offset certificates no longer satisfy street-level accountability requirements.

Deployment Without Civil Works or Permits

Grid-dependent solutions require construction permits, groundwork, and infrastructure connections before a single unit can operate. GreenAiriva is solar-powered and fully grid-independent — it integrates into existing streetscapes like public furniture, with no procedural burden and no disruption to the urban environment.

Security and Durability in Public Spaces

Value-bearing components are internally mounted and cannot be removed without damaging the enclosure — a design that deters theft and vandalism. Units are positioned at high-pollution nodes where municipal security presence already exists (intersections, facility yards, park entrances) — maximising both capture impact and operational safety.
