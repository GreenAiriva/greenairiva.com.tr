The Telecom & OOH Challenge

How GreenAiriva unlocks value from your existing urban footprint.

Microcell Concealment & Scope 1 Offset Potential

For telecom: Microcell units in urban communication networks are typically hidden to preserve aesthetics. GreenAiriva offers an integrated cavity to conceal these units while functioning as a multi-purpose platform—aesthetic, operational, and climate-positive in one. Scaled across dozens or hundreds of converted mast sites, the aggregated N₂O capture delivers enough CO₂e reduction to offset a material portion of your Scope 1 emissions. Add energy sharing (~1 MWh/year per unit), and you're funding both climate action and site operations from your existing footprint.

Energy Surplus, Zero Grid Burden

Solar-powered and energy-positive: GreenAiriva generates ~2.8 MWh/year and consumes zero grid power. Up to 1 MWh can be directed to on-site loads—cabinet climate control, backup power, billboard lighting. For telecom and OOH operators, this means operational resilience without adding load to congested infrastructure. Your sustainability narrative gains teeth: zero new capex, zero energy debt, zero environmental footprint from operation.

GreenAiriva delivers mass-based N₂O measurement, third-party verifiable, attributed to your entity at unit level. This isn't a generic credit pool; it's your reduction, in your portfolio, on your ESG disclosures. TSRS-ESG frameworks, municipal bids, and client pitches reward demonstrable climate assets. You own the data.

There's no one-size-fits-all formula. Deployment strategy is site-specific, driven by three overlapping analyses: Emission intensity — we identify high-emission corridors and traffic-dense zones using your existing traffic and air-quality data, not generic carbon baselines. Infrastructure fit — we prioritize existing mast, billboard, and street furniture points already in your portfolio, especially those in high-exposure zones (major arterials, congested districts). The result is a ranked deployment map: units placed first where climate impact is highest and operational efficiency optimal.
