The Telecom & OOH Operator's Dilemma

Net-zero ambitions meet urban infrastructure constraints.

Real Reduction Without New Capex

Your masts and billboards already exist in high-emission urban zones. But ESG frameworks increasingly reject purchased offsets—they demand documented, attributable reduction. Your existing footprint is underutilised; new site acquisition is expensive and slow. You need climate value from assets you already own.

Grid Constraints & Operational Simplicity

Telecom base stations and OOH sites often sit in congested corridors with limited grid capacity. Adding more load—or worse, becoming dependent on it—contradicts sustainability goals. You need carbon solutions that are energetically self-sufficient and don't compete with your core infrastructure.

Stakeholder Proof, Not Promises

Investors, municipal clients, and enterprise advertisers scrutinise climate claims. TSRS-ESG and Net Zero 2053 frameworks require third-party auditable data. Your competitors claim carbon-neutral; you need evidence. Unauditable, modelled, or pooled offsets no longer satisfy procurement teams or regulators.

GreenAiriva generates ~2.8 MWh annually per unit. After powering its own air-capture process (zero grid draw), up to 1 MWh can be directed to on-site loads—backup power systems, cabinet climate control, or shared billboard lighting. The specifics depend on your DC/AC infrastructure and site configuration. We assess the feasibility and set up the power-sharing arrangement during PoC scoping.

Yes. For telecom operators, we offer design variants that integrate the GreenAiriva body into or alongside existing mast structures—achieving both functional capture and visual integration. This is discussed during technical scoping; final design depends on your mast profile and site constraints.

Every unit produces mass-based measurement data—the actual weight of N₂O and F-gases captured, timestamped and unit-specific. This is third-party auditable and attributed directly to your operating entity (not pooled). Data is structured for direct input into TSRS-ESG, CBAM, and net-zero disclosure frameworks. Your auditor can review the methodology; regulators increasingly require this level of specificity.

At scale—across many masts—the aggregated CO₂e reduction has the potential to offset a significant portion of your Scope 1 emissions. We emphasize potential because this depends on your site count and local baseline emissions; it's not guaranteed, but it's a realistic outcome for large operators with hundreds of mast sites in high-emission corridors. We quantify this during the PoC briefing.
